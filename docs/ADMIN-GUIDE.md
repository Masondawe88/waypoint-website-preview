# WAYPOINT & Co. — Administrator Guide
*Plain-language guide for running the website day to day. No code required.*

The dashboard is arranged the way the house thinks: **The World** (destinations, places, plotted courses), **Sea**, **Stay**, **Air**, **Experiences**, **Journeys**, **Journal**, **Photography & film**, **Enquiries**, **Operations**, **Site settings**.

Two rules keep the brand honest, and the system enforces both:
- **Blank means "—".** Never guess a number. If a specification is blank or unverified, the website shows a quiet dash — which is correct.
- **You cannot write ownership claims.** The line under each yacht, residence or aircraft renders automatically from its *Commercial relationship*. Choose the relationship; the wording takes care of itself.

---

### 1 · Logging in
Go to `yoursite.com/studio` and sign in with your invited account. If you can't sign in, the person who manages team access can re-invite you from sanity.io/manage → Members. Editors write content; Operations additionally control "verified" switches, commercial relationships and enquiry routing.

### 2 · Editing copy
Open the entry (e.g. **Sea → ALANI**), click into the field, type, then press **Publish** (top right). Until you publish, your change is a private draft — the live site is untouched. Every field has a short description under its name telling you where it appears.

### 3 · Replacing imagery
Open **Photography & film**, find the frame by name (e.g. "Hill Inlet aerial"), upload the new image or film (films need a poster frame — the system will insist), add **Alt text** (one sentence describing the picture), turn **Placeholder** off, and publish. Every page using that frame updates automatically. Tip: the outstanding commission list is simply every entry with Placeholder still on.

### 4 · Adding a yacht
**Sea → + New yacht.** Name, web address (set once, never change), home waters, and the short design story. Leave every specification blank until confirmed — blanks render as "—". Choose the **Commercial relationship** (this writes the attribution line for you). Set **Listing status** to Listed (or Featured) and leave **Availability** as *Subject to confirmation*. Publish.

### 5 · Adding a residence
**Stay → + New residence.** Same pattern: name, destination, essence, imagery, commercial relationship, listing status. Optionally link it to a **Place** (e.g. Hamilton Island) so it joins plotted courses.

### 6 · Creating a destination
**The World → Destinations → + New.** Write the essence (the italic line), set the chart fix and season, tick which services exist there, and — most importantly — set the three **Canonical answers** (the default aircraft category, yacht and residence) and build the **Related entries** list. *The order of that list is the order of every recommendation on the site, including the Journey Engine.* Each link asks "why these connect" — write one line for future editors.

### 7 · Building a journey
**Journeys → + New.** Add legs and drag them into order — the legs *are* the plotted course the visitor sees. Each leg has a stage (Arrive / Stay / Sail / Experience / Return), a label, an optional linked entry and a one-line note. Passage details (headings, distances) stay approximate ("≈") unless Operations verifies them. Choose where it's featured (Home, Sea, Air, Engine).

### 8 · Adding waypoints (a plotted course)
First make sure the location exists under **The World → Places** (islands, beaches, anchorages, airports). Then **Plotted courses → + New waypoint**: pick the place, give it a sequence number (WPT 01, 02…), one line, and a frame. The course on the destination page draws itself from these, in sequence order.

### 9 · Publishing a journal article
**Journal → Articles → + New.** Title, kicker (e.g. "Air · Tasmania"), standfirst, hero image, body. You can drop frames into the body between paragraphs. Publish when ready; tick **Featured story** to lead the Journal section.

### 10 · Featuring content on the homepage
**Site settings** → choose the **Featured journey** and up to three **Featured journal** stories. Publish. That's the whole job.

### 11 · Reviewing enquiries
**Enquiries** shows every Journey Engine submission, newest first — the guest's details, their answers, their written story and their full plotted course. Records are read-only (they're the ledger); reply from your inbox or CRM, then update the entry's **Status** (New → In conversation → …) so the team can see where things stand. You'll also receive an email notification for each enquiry; priority rules (e.g. "urgent", travelling soon) copy the backup owner automatically.

### 12 · Publishing and rolling back
**Publish** makes a draft live (the site updates itself within a minute or two). To undo: open the entry → click the clock/history icon → choose an earlier version → **Restore** → Publish. Nothing is ever lost. To take something off the site without deleting its history, set **Listing status → Retired** — never delete.
