# WAYPOINT & Co. — Image Direction · v1.4
One governing document for all real and AI imagery.

## The feeling
Observational · quiet · place-led · natural · tactile · cinematic without drama ·
premium without staging · human without lifestyle advertising · warm but lightly graded.
An image should feel like it was *noticed*, not produced.

## Prioritise
Early and late light; natural shadow; movement through place; architecture within landscape;
water texture; aircraft and vessels **in context** (arrival, departure, at rest — never showroom);
hands, materials and details; negative space; unforced human presence (backs, distance, mid-task).

## Avoid
Generic luxury-travel advertising; champagne clichés; influencer posing; over-saturated skies;
extreme HDR; artificially perfect beaches; glossy cabin renders; implausible architecture;
groups smiling at camera; visual clutter; obvious AI symmetry; distorted machinery;
unreadable aircraft or vessel forms.

## Classification rules (hard)
REAL photography is **required** for: specific aircraft, yachts, residences, cabins, bedrooms,
deck layouts, pools, views represented as belonging to an asset, and any feature influencing
a booking decision.
AI must never invent or materially alter: asset configuration, aircraft interiors, yacht decks,
property architecture, bedroom count, pool placement, views, branding, registration, livery,
equipment or capacity.
AI **is** permitted for: destination atmosphere, journal editorial, journey concepts,
transitional scenes, future worlds, abstract moments, textures, social frames, unassigned
landscapes, emotional opening frames. AI images must never imply a named asset unless
constrained by approved source imagery of that asset.

## Technical baseline
Landscape masters 2560px+ on the long edge; portrait 1600×2000+.
Grade: restrained warmth, protected highlights, honest greens and blues.
Every published image: approved alt text, focal point set, mobile crop checked.
Internal notes (briefs, prompts, rights, IDs) never render publicly — enforced in code.

## Replacement workflow
Brief → source decided (real/AI) → draft → internal review → **accuracy review** →
rights review → approved → uploaded → focal point → mobile crop → alt text → published →
replacement date recorded if temporary. Temporary AI frames must carry a
`replacementDependency` naming the real-image requirement they hold space for.
